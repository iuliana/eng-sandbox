## Minimal Tasks for the Manager

----

## 1-1s

1-1s or One-on-ones have 2 purposes:

* Create a human connection between you and your manager
    * connection is the bedrock of strong team
    * connection leads to trust
    * trust, real trust, requires the ability and willingness to be vulnerable in front of each other
    * your manager should see you as a full human, with a life outside of work, so spend a few minutes to talk about that life when you meet
* Regular opportunity for you to speak privately with your manager about whatever needs discussing

Other things to consider:

* 1-1s should be scheduled with some predictability
* setting the agenda is not 100% manager's responsibility
    * you should put a bit of thought into what needs discussing
    * prepare form the 1-1
* they should not be status meetings!!
* as a manager reporting to senior management, check on status of critical projects or project in nascent phase

## Feedback and Workplace Guidance

* do not let all feedback add up for performance reviews
* let people know as soon as they screw up, **privately**
* the only thing worse than getting behavioral feedback is not getting it at all, or getting it only during your performance review.
* The sooner you know about your bad habits, the easier they are to correct.
* Praise should be given freely and as soon as deserved too, **publicly.**
* delivering feedback quickly is more valuable than waiting for a convenient time to say something
* public praise reinforces what good behaviour looks like
* if you are giving a presentation - ask for review and feedback
* if you've written a design doc, ask for areas of improvement
* asking for feedback from your manager is a good way to show some respect to your manager
* when it comes to your role in the company, your manager should be your no. 1 ally
* ask your manager which areas you need to focus on to get promoted
* if you are struggling with a colleague, your manager should know and should be able to help navigate the situation and find a solution
* managers should be able to help you see the value of your work, even when it is not fun or glamorous
* your manager should help you feel a sense of purpose in your dat-to-day work
* as you grow in your career the amount of feedback, good and bad will decrease
    * also the type of feedback will evolve from personal to team and strategy focused

## Training and Career Growth

* your manager is the **main liaison between you and the bureaucracy of the company**
* your manager is responsible to find you trainings and resources for your career growth
    * conference to attend
    * class to take
    * books to read
    * pointing you to an expert in the company to help you learn what you need
* However, **you are responsible**, for the most part, **for figuring out what types of training you want**
* Your manager will contribute very directly to your career growth via promotion and compensation
* Your manager will directly advocate for your promotion and getting approved
* Managers cannot guarantee promotions, but they should know the system, and tell you which areas to focus on to get promoted
* Keep in mind, at higher level promotion opportunities became rarer


### Learn how to work

## How to be Managed

* how to be managed is somewhat related to "managing your manager"
* develop a sense of authority and ownership over your own experiences and career
* **spend time thinking about what you want**
    * your manager can point you opportunities for growth
    * your manager can show you projects
    * your manager can provide feedback
    * but **they cannot read your mind** - they  can't infer what you need or makes you happy
* **you will experience uncertainty**
    * in the first 2-5 years of your career
    * after climbing the technical ladder, every 5-10 years
    * In all of this uncertainty, the only person you can rely on to pull through it is yourself. Your manager cannot do that for you. Use your manager to discover what’s possible where you are, but look to understand yourself in order to figure out where you want to go next.
* **you are responsible for yourself**
    * know yourself
    * go after what you want
    * make your own agenda for 1-1s
    * ask to work on projects you are interested in
    * if you are persistently unhappy, say something
    * if you want a raise, ask for it
    * when you want a promotion say something and find a way to get it
    * you will not always get what you want, but is the fastest way forward
* **give your manager a break**
    * your manager is human too
    * they will be stressed
    * they will say dumb things
    * they will give you work you do not want to do
    * as with any other relationship, you can only change yourself
    * if you resent your manager, you need a new one, or a new job
* **choose your managers wisely**
    * managers make a huge impact on your career - people do not quit jobs, they quit managers
    * strong managers know how to play the company game
    * strong managers and managers you like as friends and managers you respect technically are different animals
